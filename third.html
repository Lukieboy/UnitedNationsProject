How does goal 13 affect goal 14

How does climate change affect life below water?

1. Ocean Acidification: Climate change, which is driven by greenhouse gas emissions, causes the oceans to absorbe more Carbon Dioxde. This leads to ocean acidification, which can hurt many marine ecosystems and organisms, including coral reefs and shellfish. By addressing climate change and taking action (Goal 13), we can help reduce ocean acidification and help Life Below Water (Goal 14).

2. Sea-Level Rise: Goal 13 focuses on limiting global temperature rise, and this means it also aims to mitigate sea-level rise, since rising temperatures cause melting glaciers which make the sea levels go up. Rising sea levels pose a significant threat to the rise on coastal habitats, mangroves, and wetlands. This can hurt many habitats and breeding grounds for marine species. Therefore, by addressing climate change (Goal 13), we can also help reduce the sea-level rise, and preserve marine ecosystems.

3. Extreme Weather: Climate change has led to the rise of many natural disasters, including hurricanes, storms, and cyclones. These natural disasters can cause a lot of damage to maring and coastal ecosystems, and humans. This is another way how taking Climate Action (Goal 13) affects Life Below Water (Goal 14).

4. Sustainable Fisheries: Lastly, climate change also affects marine ecosystems. Climate change hurts fish populations, causes the ocean temperatures to rise, could potentially reverse the currents, and takes away nutrients from the ocean. Goal 13's actions to fight against climate change indirectly supports Goal 14's aims of sustainable fisheries, and helps Life Below Water.

How does goal 14 affect goal 13

How does life below water affect climate change?

1. Goal 14 supports the deveolopment and expansion of renewable energy sources, including offshore wnid farms, and marine solar installations. By harnessing clean and renewable energy from the oceans, this can help take Climate Action, using renewable energy instead of using fossil fuels.

2. Sustainable Fisheries: Goal 14 promotes having sustainable fisheries and protecting fish stocks. This also aligns with some of Goal 13's objectives, such as having secure food sources and livelihoods, while minimizing the environmental impact of fishing activities.

3. Climate Resilience: The main goal of Life Below Water is to enhance the resilience of marine and coastal ecosystems, which are quite vulnerable to the impacts of climate change, such as rising sea levels, ocean acidification and natural disasters. By helping to protect these ecosystems, Goal 14 helps to improve part of Goal 13, keeping healthy and resilient ecosystems.

What you can do

Walk, Bike or take Public Transport

Transportation is a very big contributer to greenhouse gas emissions and climate change. When you can, chose to take the most sustainable methods of transportation. If you can, chose to walk or bike for short distances, and use public transportation, carpooling or driving an electric/fuel efficient vehicle. This will help reduce your carbon footprint, plus it's cheaper!

Reduce energy consumption at home

Another effective way to help take action against climate change is reducing your energy consumption. Simple things such as turning off your lights and appliances when you aren't using them, can reduce the affects of climate change. Additionally, you can buy energy-efficient lighting and appliances. You can also conserve energy by adjusting your thermostat and using natural light.

Support the use of Renewable Energy

Using renewable sources is another way to help climate change. Some ways to use renewable energy are installing solar panels, or using energy providers that have renewable energy options. Additionally, you can be mindful of your energy usage, and unplug when you aren't using electricity. Using renewable and sustainable energy options can help promote Climate action in the future.

Reduce single-use plastic

Single-use plastics contribute to ocean pollution and can cause a lot of damage to marine life. Try minimizing using items such as plastic bags, plastic straws, bottles, or dispoasable cutlery. You can also use cloth bags insetead of plastic bags, resuable water bottles in stead of plastic bottles, and use paper straws. Reducing plastic waste makes the oceans cleaner, and helps preserve Life Below Water

Conserve Water

Conserving water is another important way to help Life Below Water. Conserving water helps preserve freshwaer resources, and also has a positive impact on the marine ecosystems. By taking shorter showers, fixing leaks, and using water-efficient appliances, we can maintain healthy levels in rivers, lakes, and oceans. This is very important for the survival of aquatic species.

Consume seafood responsibly

Lastly, consuming seafood responsibly can help marine ecosystems. this is because Overfishing and destructive fishing can deplete the fish populations and harm marine habitats. You can make informed choices when purchasing seafood by chosing seafood with labels like the Marine Stewardship Council (MSC) certification, or seafood from small-scale fisheries.

The Great Global Cleanup

The Great Global Cleanup is a worldwide campaign to rid the environment of waste and plastic pollution for good. Join us and help create a waste-free world. Find an event near you and help clean the world!

The Great Lakes Plastic Cleanup

The Great Lakes Plastic Cleanup is a first-of-its-kind initiative using innovative technology to quickly remove plastics at marinas from Lake Ontario to Lake Superior and everywhere in between. The litter collected will be analyzed and valuable plastics will be recovered, to be redirected back into the recycling system and creating reuse opportunities. Throughout, we’ll be working with lake communities and businesses to keep plastics out of our environment and waterways to protect the Great Lakes region, now and for future generations.
